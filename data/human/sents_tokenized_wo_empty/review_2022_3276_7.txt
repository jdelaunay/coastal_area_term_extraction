Serpulid reefs can be divided roughly into seven groups , according to the building modality and the type of habitat they occupy : ( i ) pseudocolonies ; ( ii ) littoral belts ; ( iii ) subtidal to deep - water reefs ; ( iv ) reefs in coastal lakes and harbours ; ( v ) brackish water reefs ; ( vi ) tapestries in freshwater caves ; ( vii ) biostalactites inside marine caves .
